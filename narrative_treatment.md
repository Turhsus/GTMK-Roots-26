# The Narrative

One idea I had about the narrative of this game was to make it a sweet coming of age Story.

I think over the course of the game, time is passing and your little adventurer is growing up. All the elements of gameplay should help fit this theme. 

"Mom, where did dad go? Why didn't he come back from his adventure?"
"Mom, I want to buy you a house on the mountain top when I grow up!"

The little adventurer will go on quests. If he fails a quest, it doesn't trigger a game over state. Instead the game keeps going and plays a little vignette about failure.

"Failure isn't the end little one. It's a lesson! You think the mighty lion has won every hunt its gone on? Every bird has flown every time it took to the skies? No, everything in life takes practice, and failing at something is just a lesson on how to do better next time."
"So I can be an adventurer again?"
"As long as you dream of it, you can! As long as your will remains, you can do anything you put your mind to. It just might take some time to get there."
"I love you mom."
"I love you too, little one."

**Players at this point can select a perk for their adventurer!**
These perks are things like:
- "Your little one has learned from his past mistakes and learned to forage on his quests! +1 Food for each quest."
- "Your little one has learned to be crafty. There is a 10% chance a utility item is not used on quests."

Then, after a certain number of days have past, he becomes a full fledged adventurer. There will be a last boss quest... And then one more after, an easier one, and when it comes time to pack his bag...

"Mom! I can pack my bag! I'm heading out today, I can do at least that much!"
"Oh... I know honey, I just... I'm worried about you is all."
"I know mom, but... I got this. You prepared me for this after all."
"I know... but, wait! Did you get your health potion? And your torch? You know--"
"MOM. Quit stalling! I have everything... Its time for me to go now! I'll write I promise."

*They hug*

"Bye honey, write home lots."

"Yes, yes of couse. I'm off!"

*The adventurer leaves*

"I hope he's going to be okay... but I've done all I've can."
**Tears well up in the mother's eyes**
"But if anyone can do it, <adventurer> can. I must do what I have always done and have faith in the child I raised!" 

*The door bursts open*

"Oh, and mom, when I make it big I'm going to buy you that house on the mountain!"

*Fin*

End the game on the freeze frame there! Maybe have different epilogues depending on how many lessons were learned and how many quests were completed by the time limit.
